## Problem Statement

Thai Meet is a Thailand-focused Android/iOS dating app for Thai local women and foreign tourists who want to move from in-app chat to LINE or Facebook without awkward workarounds. In existing products, ThaiFriendly may hide LINE IDs sent as text, while Tinder/Bumble make external contact sharing feel policy-risky or socially uncomfortable.

The first product thesis is not "make contact sharing easy for everyone." The sharper thesis is: Thai local women should be able to start fresh with a new Public Meet ID and share LINE/Facebook only when they explicitly choose, using a controlled Contact Card instead of leaking raw IDs in chat text.

## Demand Evidence

Current evidence from the session:

- ThaiFriendly: LINE ID sent as plain chat text can be hidden, forcing users into workaround behavior.
- Tinder/Bumble: users may feel external contact sharing is policy-risky or unsafe.
- Founder hypothesis: Bangkok/Pattaya Thai local women and foreign tourists need a dating flow that makes LINE/Facebook transition explicit, fast, and controlled.

Evidence still missing:

- No specific Thai local woman has been named yet.
- No interview quotes, conversion numbers, waitlist deposits, community posts, or review excerpts were provided in this session.
- The main risk is that the pain is real for some users, but not strong enough to make Thai local women join a new app first.

## Status Quo

Users currently work around the problem by:

- Typing LINE IDs in ways that avoid hiding or moderation.
- Delaying LINE/Facebook exchange until trust is built inside the app.
- Moving to other social channels manually after multiple chat messages.
- Staying on incumbent apps despite poor contact-sharing UX because the user pool is already there.

The workaround cost is friction, uncertainty, and lower trust: tourists want fast coordination during a short trip, while Thai local women need control over when an external contact becomes visible.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

Initial target user:

- Thai local women in Bangkok and Pattaya, especially users who receive many messages or want separation between dating identity and private LINE/Facebook identity.

Secondary user:

- Foreign tourists from the US, Europe, China, Korea, and Japan, ages 20-50, visiting Thailand and wanting a simple way to connect with Thai local users.

Narrowest wedge:

- Public Meet ID + controlled LINE/Facebook Contact Card sharing.

The first wedge message:

> Start fresh with a new Public Meet ID. Share LINE/Facebook only when you choose. No hidden LINE ID text, no awkward workaround.

## Constraints

- Android/iOS only; no consumer web app for MVP.
- Flutter app, NestJS backend, PostgreSQL/Prisma, Redis, object storage, FCM, and AdMob remain the intended stack.
- The user selected Approach B: a PRD-scaled full MVP, so the first build includes more than the contact-card core.
- Scope must still be disciplined: advanced AI recommendation, live streaming, voice/video calling, strict identity verification, full premium subscription UX, and consumer web are excluded.
- Safety must be low-friction but real: block/report, contact exchange logs, internal userId continuity, and admin review are required.

## Premises

1. Thai local women are the supply side to seed first; without them, tourist demand will churn quickly.
2. "Easy contact sharing" must be framed as "controlled contact sharing" for Thai local women.
3. Public Meet ID regeneration is not a gimmick; it is the trust and reset mechanism that makes external contact sharing less scary.
4. A PRD-scaled full MVP can be justified only if it preserves learning gates and does not turn every PRD feature into a P0 launch blocker.
5. Advertising should be included in the full MVP, but revenue optimization should not outrank activation and contact-sharing validation.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Contact Card Validation MVP

Summary: Build only Public ID, minimum profile, Discover, chat, and LINE/Facebook Contact Card sharing.

Effort: M

Risk: Medium

Pros:

- Fastest path to testing the core wedge.
- Keeps engineering focus on the differentiated behavior.
- Makes failure easier to diagnose.

Cons:

- May feel too thin compared with incumbent dating apps.
- Does not validate ad monetization, broader activity loops, or admin operations.
- Could under-serve early users who expect a complete dating app.

### Approach B: PRD Scaled Full MVP

Summary: Build the PRD as a narrowed but launchable app: auth, Public ID, profiles/photos, Discover/search, chat, LINE/Facebook Contact Cards, basic ad monetization, 5-language UI foundation, low-friction safety, admin MVP, and analytics.

Effort: XL

Risk: High

Pros:

- Closer to a real app users can keep using after first contact exchange.
- Includes operational tools needed for safety, ads, moderation, and learning.
- Supports the founder's chosen direction without making the MVP feel like a throwaway prototype.

Cons:

- Higher chance of overbuilding before demand proof.
- More moving parts make launch delays likely.
- If activation fails, it may be hard to know whether the issue is supply, UX, trust, ads, language, or distribution.

### Approach C: Manual Market Test Before App Build

Summary: Recruit a small group of Thai local women and tourists and manually test the promise: fresh identity plus controlled LINE/Facebook sharing.

Effort: S

Risk: Low

Pros:

- Cheapest way to validate messaging and user fear.
- Could reveal which safety controls matter before coding.
- Useful for collecting exact onboarding copy and screenshots.

Cons:

- Does not produce a shippable app.
- Manual operations can distort behavior.
- Founder selected a more product-forward approach.

## Recommended Approach

Proceed with Approach B, but treat it as a staged full MVP rather than a flat all-at-once build.

The product should ship as a coherent dating app, but the internal priority order must protect the wedge:

1. Public ID + ID image + profile foundation.
2. Discover/search + profile detail.
3. Chat + LINE/Facebook Contact Card.
4. Report/block + admin visibility for user, Public ID, reports, and contact exchanges.
5. Analytics for activation, first message, contact share, and Public ID regeneration.
6. Ads and rewards after the core contact-sharing loop is stable.
7. Full polish for 5 language packs before public launch, but development can start with Korean/English plus key architecture for all five.

Approach B is approved by the founder in this session, with one condition: it must keep the Thai local women's trust proposition at the center.

### Launch Gates

Approach B remains the approved MVP scope, but it must not be executed as one undifferentiated launch block. Ship and validate it through explicit gates:

**Gate 0: Trust Loop**

- Public Meet ID creation and active identity selection.
- Minimum profile and generated ID image fallback.
- Discover grid/list enough to find real people.
- Chat room creation and text messaging.
- LINE Contact Card sharing from chat.
- Report/block for profile, chat, and contact card.
- Core analytics: onboarding complete, profile impression, first message, LINE share click, LINE card sent, report, block, Public ID generated.

Gate 0 success means Thai local women can safely try the core promise: fresh public identity plus controlled LINE sharing.

Gate 0 architecture decision:

- Contact Card messages must reference `ContactExchange` by id only.
- Raw LINE ID, Facebook URL, QR payload, or encrypted external contact fields must not be copied into `ChatMessage` payloads.
- `ContactExchange` is the permission, audit, revoke, report, and admin-inspection boundary.
- Rendering a Contact Card must re-check room membership, block/report visibility, exchange status, and contact revocation before decrypting or opening contact details.

Gate 0 Public ID safety decision:

- `PublicIdentity` is the public-facing persona used for discovery, profile display, and identity reset UX.
- Internal `User.id` is the safety ledger for reports, blocks, moderation actions, login/device history, spam risk, and abuse prevention.
- `Block` must default to `blockerUserId -> blockedUserId`, while UI may also record the visible `PublicIdentity` that triggered the action.
- `Report` must store both `reportedUserId` and `reportedPublicIdentityId`.
- A new Public Meet ID must not bypass existing user-level blocks, report risk, moderation state, or spam penalties.
- Discover ranking may give a freshness boost to a new Public ID, but must also apply internal user-level risk penalties.

Gate 0 chat identity decision:

- `ChatRoomParticipant` must store `publicIdentityIdAtCreation` for each participant.
- `ChatMessage` must store `senderPublicIdentityId` as the identity visible at send time.
- Existing chat rooms must not automatically switch to a user's new active Public ID.
- New Public ID activity should create or use rooms scoped to the new visible identity.
- Archiving a Public ID can archive or separate only rooms tied to that Public ID snapshot, while user-level reports and blocks still apply globally through `User.id`.

Gate 0 contact provider decision:

- Data model and backend APIs must be provider-agnostic via `ContactProvider`.
- Supported enum values should include at least `LINE` and `FACEBOOK`; future values may include `KAKAOTALK` and `WHATSAPP`.
- Gate 0 UI, QA, copy, analytics, and operational runbooks are LINE-first.
- Gate 0 flags: `CONTACT_PROVIDER_LINE_ENABLED=true`, `CONTACT_PROVIDER_FACEBOOK_ENABLED=false`.
- Gate 1 enables Facebook without changing the core contact-exchange schema.

Reward and entitlement decision:

- Rewarded Ad, admin grant, and future purchase benefits must use an append-only reward/entitlement ledger rather than mutable counters on `User`.
- Reward grant events require an `idempotencyKey` to prevent duplicate AdMob callback rewards.
- Reward consumption must be transactional and auditable.
- Gate 2 can hide reward UI until ads are enabled, but schema and service boundaries should support `RewardLedger` / `RewardConsumption` from the start.
- Reward types should include `EXTRA_MESSAGE`, `EXTRA_LINE_SHARE`, `EXTRA_FACEBOOK_SHARE`, `BOOST`, and future visitor/list unlocks.

**Gate 1: Full MVP Alpha**

- Facebook Contact Card sharing.
- Admin MVP for user search, Public ID history, reports, blocks, and contact exchanges.
- Photo upload, search filters, and profile detail stabilization.
- Localization foundation for th/en/ko/zh-Hans/ja, with launch polish prioritized by alpha audience.
- Push notifications without exposing external contact details.

Gate 1 success means the product is operationally supportable and complete enough for a controlled alpha.

**Gate 2: Monetization MVP**

- AdMob placement configuration.
- Rewarded Ad reward ledger.
- Ad event analytics and revenue event capture.
- Frequency caps and Remote Config/Feature Flag controls.
- Clear rule: no ads inside chat detail and no ads interrupting Contact Card sharing.

Gate 2 success means monetization is measurable without damaging the Thai local women's trust loop.

## Success Criteria

Activation and trust:

- 70%+ of social-login starters complete onboarding.
- New users reach Discover within 90 seconds.
- 30%+ of activated users send a first message.
- 20%+ of chat creators attempt LINE or Facebook Contact Card sharing.
- Thai local women can create or switch Public ID without losing internal safety, report, or block continuity.

Contact-sharing validation:

- LINE/Facebook Contact Card can be sent in two taps from chat after setup.
- Contact Card contains clear Open, Copy, Report, and Block actions.
- Raw external contact details are never included in push notifications.

Operational readiness:

- Admin can search users by internal userId, Public ID, and provider identity.
- Admin can view reports, blocks, Public ID history, and contact-exchange events.
- Analytics track login, onboarding, Discover impressions, first message, contact share, ad events, Public ID generation, report, and block.

MVP scope guard:

- Swipe/Hot can exist only if it does not delay the contact-card loop.
- Premium subscription UI is Phase 2; only database preparation is included.
- AI recommendation, auto-translation, voice/video, live streaming, and strict identity verification are excluded.

## Engineering Test Matrix

Gate 0: Trust Loop

- Auth/onboarding unit and integration tests:
  - First social login creates `User`, `UserIdentity`, initial `PublicIdentity`, and `activePublicIdentityId`.
  - Repeated provider login resolves the existing user and does not create duplicates.
  - Missing or invalid provider profile data fails loudly with a localized retry path.
- Public ID tests:
  - New Public ID creation does not delete or mutate previous identities.
  - Archived IDs remain visible to admin and remain tied to historical rooms.
  - New Public ID does not bypass user-level block, report risk, moderation state, or spam penalties.
- Chat identity tests:
  - `ChatRoomParticipant.publicIdentityIdAtCreation` is set for each participant.
  - `ChatMessage.senderPublicIdentityId` is stored at send time.
  - Switching active Public ID does not mutate old room display identity.
- ContactExchange tests:
  - Contact Card messages store `contactExchangeId` only.
  - Raw LINE/Facebook values are never copied into `ChatMessage` payloads.
  - Missing `ExternalContact` shows setup flow and creates no message.
  - Revoked or hidden exchanges render as unavailable.
  - Blocked receivers cannot open contact details.
- Safety tests:
  - Report stores both `reportedUserId` and `reportedPublicIdentityId`.
  - Block defaults to internal user-level blocking.
  - Discover excludes or downranks blocked/reported users according to policy.
- Gate 0 E2E smoke:
  - Login -> Public ID -> Discover -> Chat -> LINE Contact Card -> receiver opens card -> report/block path.

Gate 1: Full MVP Alpha

- Facebook provider tests mirror LINE ContactExchange behavior.
- Admin tests cover user search, Public ID history, reports, blocks, contact exchanges, and restricted sensitive contact visibility.
- Photo upload tests cover presigned URL creation, upload success, failed upload, missing image, and fallback to generated ID image.
- Localization tests cover th/en/ko/zh-Hans/ja key completeness and layout smoke on small screens.
- Push tests verify that LINE/Facebook contact values never appear in notification payloads.

Gate 2: Monetization MVP

- Reward ledger tests:
  - Ad reward grant is idempotent by `idempotencyKey`.
  - Reward consumption is transactional and cannot go negative.
  - Expired rewards cannot be consumed.
  - Admin grants and future purchase grants share the same ledger path.
- Ad analytics tests cover request, impression, click, rewarded completion, fail, and revenue-paid events.
- Frequency cap tests verify Remote Config or Feature Flag values are enforced.
- Chat detail tests verify ads are never rendered inside active chat detail and never interrupt Contact Card sharing.

## Observability And Audit Requirements

Day-1 audit events must exist for every sensitive trust, identity, safety, contact, and reward action:

- `public_identity.generated`
- `public_identity.activated`
- `public_identity.archived`
- `chat_room.created`
- `message.sent`
- `contact_exchange.created`
- `contact_exchange.viewed`
- `contact_exchange.revoked`
- `contact_exchange.hidden_by_report`
- `report.submitted`
- `block.created`
- `moderation.action_applied`
- `reward.granted`
- `reward.consumed`

Audit events must include enough context to reconstruct an incident without exposing raw LINE IDs, Facebook URLs, QR payloads, or decrypted external contact details in logs.

Day-1 dashboard panels:

- Trust Loop:
  - onboarding completion rate
  - first message rate
  - LINE contact share click rate
  - LINE contact card open rate
  - reports and blocks per 100 contact exchanges
- Safety:
  - top reported users
  - repeated Public ID regeneration by user
  - contact exchange reports
  - blocks after contact open
- Ops:
  - failed social login rate
  - failed contact decrypt/open rate
  - duplicate reward callbacks prevented
  - push notification failures

CloudWatch or application logs alone are insufficient for trust and safety operations. Product analytics and audit logging are separate responsibilities: analytics explains funnel behavior, while audit logging explains who did what, when, under which identity and permission state.

## Feature Flag Matrix And Rollout Sequence

Feature flags must exist before alpha rollout because mobile releases are slower to roll back than server-side configuration.

Core flags:

| Flag | Gate | Default | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|---|
| `PUBLIC_ID_REGEN_ENABLED` | 0 | true for alpha | Allow users to create a new Public Meet ID. |
| `PUBLIC_ID_ARCHIVE_ENABLED` | 0 | true for alpha | Allow users to archive previous public identities. |
| `CONTACT_PROVIDER_LINE_ENABLED` | 0 | true | Enable LINE contact setup and sharing. |
| `CONTACT_PROVIDER_FACEBOOK_ENABLED` | 1 | false | Enable Facebook contact setup and sharing after LINE stabilizes. |
| `CONTACT_SHARE_WARNING_ONCE` | 0 | true | Show first-share warning before external contact details are shared. |
| `REQUIRE_MATCH_TO_CHAT` | 0 | false | Keep direct chat open for MVP unless abuse requires tightening. |
| `REQUIRE_MATCH_TO_SHARE_CONTACT` | 0 | false | Keep contact sharing available in chat unless abuse requires tightening. |
| `CHAT_DETAIL_ADS_ENABLED` | 2 | false | Must remain false for MVP unless explicitly revisited. |
| `REWARDED_ADS_ENABLED` | 2 | false | Enable rewarded ads only after trust loop metrics are healthy. |
| `STRICT_MODERATION_ENABLED` | 1 | false | Keep low-friction safety posture unless abuse patterns require stricter checks. |
| `AUTO_HIDE_REPORTED_PROFILE_THRESHOLD` | 1 | 5 | Hide profiles after repeated reports. |
| `AUTO_SUSPEND_REPORT_THRESHOLD` | 1 | 10 | Suspend accounts after stronger repeated report signals. |

Rollout sequence:

1. Apply backward-compatible DB migrations with risky features defaulted off.
2. Deploy backend services and admin/audit visibility.
3. Verify audit events and dashboards in staging.
4. Ship mobile internal alpha build.
5. Enable Gate 0 flags for internal testers.
6. Enable Gate 0 flags for invited alpha users in one launch city.
7. Enable Gate 1 Facebook only after LINE contact setup/share/open/report flows are stable.
8. Enable Gate 2 rewarded ads only after trust metrics are healthy and support load is manageable.

Rollback posture:

- Disable provider flags to stop new contact exchanges without deleting existing history.
- Disable Public ID regeneration if abuse spikes, while keeping existing identities readable.
- Disable rewarded ads without deleting reward ledger history.
- Keep admin/audit access independent from user-facing feature flags.

## Repository Structure Decision

Thai Meet should start as a monorepo. This keeps the Flutter app, NestJS API, infra, contracts, and product docs together while the product surface is still changing quickly.

Recommended structure:

```text
thai-meet/
  apps/
    mobile/          # Flutter Android/iOS app
    api/             # NestJS modular monolith
    admin/           # Gate 1 admin web, added when admin build begins
  packages/
    api-contracts/   # OpenAPI/schema/types, added when contracts need sharing
  infra/
    docker/
    aws/
  docs/
    designs/
    prd/
  benchmark/
```

Rules:

- Do not split mobile and API into separate repositories during Gate 0.
- Keep shared contract generation explicit; avoid early package sprawl.
- `apps/api` owns Prisma schema, migrations, and backend modules.
- `apps/mobile` consumes API contracts but should not duplicate backend validation rules by hand where generated schemas are available.
- `docs/prd` should hold source PRDs and review artifacts that the team needs to see in-repo.
- `benchmark` remains for competitor/source material and should not be mixed with implementation assets.

## API Contract Decision

Thai Meet should use OpenAPI as the source of truth for mobile/backend API contracts.

Contract flow:

```text
NestJS DTOs and decorators
  -> generated OpenAPI JSON
  -> generated Dart API client/types
  -> Flutter app consumes generated contracts
```

Contract requirements:

- `apps/api` owns DTOs, validation, OpenAPI generation, and backend contract tests.
- `apps/mobile` must consume generated API clients/types where practical instead of hand-writing duplicate request/response models.
- `packages/api-contracts` may hold generated OpenAPI artifacts and shared generated clients once generation becomes useful.
- Contract generation must run in CI once both apps exist.

APIs that must be covered by generated contracts:

- Auth and social login.
- PublicIdentity creation, activation, archive, and history.
- Profile and photo upload setup.
- Discover/search.
- ChatRoom and ChatMessage.
- ExternalContact and ContactExchange.
- Report and Block.
- RewardLedger and reward consumption.
- AdsConfig and ad event ingestion.
- Admin-visible audit and moderation APIs.

## Database Constraints And Index Matrix

Gate 0 schema work must include constraints and indexes up front. These are part of correctness, not later optimization.

Recommended constraints and indexes:

```text
UserIdentity:
  unique(provider, providerUserId)
  index(userId)

PublicIdentity:
  unique(publicId)
  unique(handle) where handle is not null
  index(userId, status)
  index(status, createdAt)

ExternalContact:
  unique(userId, provider) where isActive=true
  index(userId, provider)
  index(publicIdentityId)

ChatRoomParticipant:
  unique(roomId, userId, publicIdentityIdAtCreation)
  index(userId, publicIdentityIdAtCreation)
  index(roomId)

ChatMessage:
  index(roomId, createdAt)
  index(senderUserId, createdAt)
  index(senderPublicIdentityId)
  ensure contact-card messages reference contactExchangeId and do not duplicate raw contact payload

ContactExchange:
  index(roomId, createdAt)
  index(senderUserId, receiverUserId)
  index(contactId)
  index(status, createdAt)

Block:
  unique(blockerUserId, blockedUserId)
  index(blockedUserId)

Report:
  index(reportedUserId, createdAt)
  index(reportedPublicIdentityId, createdAt)
  index(reporterUserId, createdAt)

RewardLedger:
  unique(idempotencyKey) where idempotencyKey is not null
  index(userId, type, expiresAt)
  index(publicIdentityId)

RewardConsumption:
  index(rewardLedgerId)
  index(actionType, actionId)

AuditEvent:
  index(actorUserId, createdAt)
  index(targetUserId, createdAt)
  index(eventType, createdAt)
```

Transaction boundaries:

- Social login must create or resolve `User`, `UserIdentity`, initial `PublicIdentity`, and `activePublicIdentityId` atomically.
- Public ID activation must atomically update `User.activePublicIdentityId` and identity status rules.
- Contact sharing must atomically create `ContactExchange` and the corresponding `ChatMessage`.
- Reward grant and reward consumption must be transactional and idempotent.
- Report/block actions must write audit events in the same logical operation.
