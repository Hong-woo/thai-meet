# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Thai Meet serves Thai local women in Bangkok, Pattaya, and nearby travel-heavy areas first. They want fast discovery and chat while keeping private LINE or Facebook identity under their control. Secondary users are foreign tourists and residents from the US, Europe, China, Korea, and Japan who need a simple, language-tolerant way to meet Thai locals and move to LINE only when both sides choose it.

## Product Purpose

Thai Meet is a Thailand-focused Android/iOS dating app. Gate 0 proves the Trust Loop: mock login, Public Meet ID, Discover, Swipe, Chat, LINE ContactExchange, redacted Contact Card, revoke, report, and block. Success means users can discover quickly, start a chat, exchange a LINE contact card explicitly, stop access later, and avoid raw contact leakage in chat, push, logs, or safety previews.

## Brand Personality

Warm, direct, protective. The product should feel socially fast and mobile-native, but not reckless. It should be photo-forward like a dating app while making trust controls visible enough that Thai local women feel able to reset public identity, decide when to share LINE, and report or block without friction.

## Anti-references

Avoid a dark Tinder clone, a raw contact-sharing shortcut, a generic SaaS dashboard, a tourism brochure, or a verification-heavy identity product. Do not let LINE green become the brand primary. Do not hide trust behind legal copy, long onboarding, or admin-like settings.

## Design Principles

1. Swipe is a primary path, but list and search must stay practical for ThaiFriendly and MEEFF-style browsing.
2. Trust is visible in the working UI: Public Meet ID, Contact Cards, redaction, revoke, report, and block appear where decisions happen.
3. LINE sharing is an explicit ContactExchange object, not copied chat text.
4. Thai local woman first: protect private identity, make reset and block easy, and keep tone calm without slowing discovery.
5. Mobile density beats landing-page polish: each screen should support repeated thumb use, fast scanning, and clear active states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Primary tap targets should be at least 44px with safe-area spacing. Designs must account for Thai, English, Korean, Chinese, and Japanese labels, reduced motion, color-blind users, and users who avoid profile photos early in the flow.
